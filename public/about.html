About Union Cafe

Not just a coffee shop — a community hub focused on quality, service, and genuine hospitality.

Our Story

In 2021, after working in Lynn’s restaurant industry for more than a decade, Charbel Wehbe saved every dollar to open his dream cafe. Union Cafe replaced a former Dunkin’ Donuts with something the community truly needed: a local, owner-operated gathering place.

Together with his fiancé Sulma Villanueva, Charbel built Union Cafe on three principles: quality over everything, service like family, and community first. The result? A welcoming space where locals gather, conversations thrive, and everyone feels at home.

What Sets Us Apart

  • Real eggs in every breakfast sandwich — no frozen patties.
  • Desserts from our family bakery — homemade, never store-bought.
  • Charbel greets customers personally and knows regulars by name.
  • Fast, friendly, and consistent service — even during the morning rush.
  • A spotless cafe with warm, bright, and inviting energy.

Quality, Service, Consistency

“I like to feed people,” Charbel says. “We want to serve people how they want to be served.” That dedication shows up in every order, every smile, and every detail — from spotless counters to the $50–$80 in tips staff bring home daily because guests feel the care.

Union Cafe isn’t driven by profit margins or corporate checklists. It’s powered by passion, pride, and the belief that Lynn deserves a better coffee experience. We’ll always choose quality, service, consistency, and community over shortcuts.

Quality

We never compromise on ingredients or preparation. Fresh, homemade, and made-to-order every single time.

Service

Meet Charbel Wehbe

“I got into the restaurant industry because I like to feed people. After working long hours in a Lynn pizza shop, I saved every dollar to open Union Cafe. This is my dream — to serve my community with quality, care, and consistency.”

“I don’t care about money. I care about quality and service. That’s why our grand opening featured 99-cent bagels with cream cheese. That’s why I personally greet every customer. That’s why we’ll keep doing this forever. This is more than a business — it’s a calling.”

“Thank you to the whole community and each customer that supports us. And a special thank you to my fiancé, Sulma, who has always been there for me.”
