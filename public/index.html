Our Story

The Wakefield café built on breakfast joy.

Omelette Headquarters began as a Lake Quannapowitt pop-up and grew into the town's beloved brunch clubhouse. We source from Middlesex County farms, churn our own cultured butter, and weave playful Boston energy into every plate.

Mornings start with espresso tastings, afternoons flow with mocktails, and Sundays are for neighborhood vinyl swaps. If it tastes like sunshine and feels like community, it belongs on our menu.

Chef Dana Avery

Founder & Executive Omelette Artist

Our Standards

What makes every plate Omelette HQ.

  • Farm eggs, always: Pasture-raised deliveries from Brookford Farm and Kimball Fruit Farm.
  • House-fermented sauces: Pineapple fire relish, chili honey, and pickled fresnos on tap.
  • Compost-first kitchen: Spent grounds and veg trim fuel our community garden beds.
  • Hospitality at heart: Vinyl-spun brunches, mocktail pairings, and neighbor discounts.

Experience

Every visit feels like a mini vacation.

From the pastel palette to the curated playlists, Omelette Headquarters is designed for lingering. Choose a counter seat to chat with the chefs or settle into the sunroom for a long brunch with friends.

Egg Bar Theatre

Watch omelettes folded to order with fillings like maple-glazed bacon, lemony greens, and house-fermented hot sauces.

Slow Coffee Counter

Sip maple cortados, nitro cold brew, and seasonal espresso tonics roasted with our Wakefield partners at Perch Coffee.

Vinyl Brunch Sessions

Local DJs spin mellow sunshine tracks on Saturdays, while Sundays feature neighbor-curated vinyl listening parties.
